Quick takeaways

  • Definition: G = H - TS.
  • Key criterion (constant T,P): ΔG < 0 spontaneous, ΔG = 0 equilibrium.
  • Second-law link (constant T,P): ΔG = -TΔSuniverse.
  • Reactions: ΔG = ΔG° + RT ln Q and ΔG° = -RT ln K.
  • Maximum useful work: wuseful,max = -ΔG.

Definitions

Gibbs free energy is the thermodynamic potential that is most useful when a system can exchange heat with a reservoir and do expansion work against a constant external pressure.

State functions you already know

  • Internal energy: U
  • Enthalpy: H = U + PV
  • Entropy: S

Gibbs free energy

G = H - TS = U + PV - TS

Units: joules (J) or kJ per mole for reactions.

Connection to the Second Law

The Second Law is usually written for the “universe” (system + surroundings):

ΔSuniverse = ΔSsystem + ΔSsurroundings ≥ 0

Gibbs free energy becomes the “go-to” criterion because, under the common constraint of constant temperature (T) and constant pressure (P), the Second Law can be rewritten in terms of ΔG.

Derivation (constant T,P; only PV work)

If the surroundings are a thermal reservoir at temperature T, then:

  • At constant pressure, the heat exchanged by the system is qp = ΔH.
  • The surroundings’ entropy change is ΔSsurroundings = -qp/T = -ΔH/T.

Therefore:

ΔSuniverse = ΔSsystem - ΔH/T

Multiply by -T:

-TΔSuniverse = ΔH - TΔSsystem = ΔG

Result (constant T,P): ΔG = -TΔSuniverse. So the Second Law condition ΔSuniverse ≥ 0 is equivalent to ΔG ≤ 0.

See also: Second Law of Thermodynamics (entropy generation and why real processes satisfy Sgen ≥ 0).

Spontaneity and equilibrium (constant T,P)

Because ΔG = -TΔSuniverse under constant T and P:

  • ΔG < 0 means ΔSuniverse > 0: spontaneous.
  • ΔG = 0 means ΔSuniverse = 0: equilibrium (reversible limit).
  • ΔG > 0 means ΔSuniverse < 0: not spontaneous as written.

Temperature dependence

Using ΔG = ΔH - TΔS, increasing T makes the entropy term more important.

If ΔH < 0 and ΔS > 0

Always spontaneous (ΔG negative at all T).

If ΔH > 0 and ΔS < 0

Never spontaneous (ΔG positive at all T).

If ΔH < 0 and ΔS < 0

Spontaneous at low T.

If ΔH > 0 and ΔS > 0

Spontaneous at high T.

Reactions: Q, K, and ΔG

For a chemical reaction at a fixed temperature, the free energy change depends on how far you are from equilibrium:

ΔG = ΔG° + RT ln Q
  • R is the gas constant.
  • Q is the reaction quotient (activities; often approximated with concentrations/pressures).
  • ΔG° uses a defined standard state (the ° symbol matters).

Equilibrium constant

At equilibrium, ΔG = 0 and Q = K, so:

ΔG° = -RT ln K

Big K means products are favored and ΔG° is strongly negative.

Useful work and batteries

At constant T and P, the maximum non-expansion (useful) work a process can deliver equals the drop in Gibbs free energy:

wuseful,max = -ΔG

Electrochemistry shortcut

For an electrochemical cell:

ΔG = -nFE

Here n is moles of electrons, F is Faraday’s constant, and E is the cell potential.

Worked examples

1) Spontaneous at 298 K?

A process has ΔH = -40 kJ and ΔS = -120 J/K at T = 298 K.

ΔG = ΔH - TΔS = -40 kJ - 298(-0.120 kJ/K) ≈ -4.2 kJ

Since ΔG < 0, it’s spontaneous (at these conditions).

2) Find K from ΔG°

For a reaction, ΔG° = +5.7 kJ/mol at 298 K. What is K?

K = exp(-ΔG°/(RT)) ≈ exp(-5700/(8.314·298)) ≈ 0.10

3) Second-law interpretation

If ΔG = -2.5 kJ at T = 300 K, then:

ΔSuniverse = -ΔG/T ≈ (2500 J)/(300 K) ≈ 8.3 J/K

The universe’s entropy increases, consistent with the Second Law.

4) Maximum electrical work

If a cell transfers n = 2 moles of electrons per mole of reaction and has E = 1.10 V, then:

ΔG = -nFE ≈ -(2)(96485 C/mol)(1.10 J/C) ≈ -212 kJ/mol

Common pitfalls

  • Confusing ΔG and ΔG°: ΔG depends on current conditions (Q); ΔG° is tied to the standard state.
  • Forgetting the constraint: the criterion ΔG < 0 is specifically for constant T and P.
  • Mixing up “spontaneous” and “fast”: thermodynamics predicts direction; kinetics sets rate.
  • Activities vs concentrations: equilibrium expressions are exact in terms of activity; concentrations are an approximation.

Summary

  • G = H - TS = U + PV - TS
  • ΔG = ΔH - TΔS
  • ΔG = -TΔSuniverse (constant T,P; only PV work)
  • ΔG = ΔG° + RT ln Q
  • ΔG° = -RT ln K
  • wuseful,max = -ΔG
