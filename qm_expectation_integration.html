Quantum Mechanics — Expectation Values & Integration

A self-contained note derived from an original handwritten question.

Original Question

Is this correct?

Extracted From the Handwritten Image

\[ \hat H\psi = E\psi \] \[ \Rightarrow \; \psi\cdot \hat H\psi = \psi\cdot E\psi = E|\psi|^2 \] \[ \langle \psi|\hat H|\psi\rangle = E|\psi|^2 \]

Is It Correct?

No. The final expression confuses ordinary multiplication with a quantum inner product and omits the normalization factor.
\[ \langle \psi|\hat H|\psi\rangle = \int \psi^*(x)\,\hat H\psi(x)\,dx \] \[ \hat H\psi = E\psi \;\Rightarrow\; \langle \psi|\hat H|\psi\rangle = E\langle \psi|\psi\rangle \] \[ \text{If } \langle \psi|\psi\rangle = 1, \quad \langle \psi|\hat H|\psi\rangle = E \]

Meaning of Integration

Integration means adding up infinitely many tiny contributions across a continuous variable.

\[ P(a\le x\le b)=\int_a^b |\psi(x)|^2\,dx \] \[ \langle A\rangle = \int \psi^*(x)\,\hat A\psi(x)\,dx \]

These integrals represent continuous versions of weighted averages.
