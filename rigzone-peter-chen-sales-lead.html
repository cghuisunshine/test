[Transition to Slide 8]

SLIDE 8 — COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE

Let me make the competitive case.

Our direct online competitors — Amazon and Newegg — have selection and price. But they cannot offer hands-on testing. You cannot pick up a mouse on Amazon before you buy it. Full stop.

Best Buy and Canada Computers are the closest physical alternatives. But as you can see in the table, they stock mainstream brands only. They do not carry Wooting, Lamzu, or Artisan. Their staff are generalists. They have zero community curation.

RigZone is the only retailer that combines all six criteria on this table: niche enthusiast products, in-store demo and testing, expert gamer staff, community-driven curation, easy local returns, and competitive pricing.

Our moat is the in-store testing experience — physical presence combined with community trust creates a flywheel that gets stronger over time. And yes, we acknowledge the known risk on the right side of the slide: online retailers could eventually expand their niche SKUs. Our answer to that is simple — community relationships and in-person demos are advantages no website can copy, no matter how many SKUs they add.
